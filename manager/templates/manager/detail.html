Tips & Info

General

  • Click the banner at the top of the screen to return to the home page
  • You can search for a Classic or a Head-to-Head league but only the top 50 players will be shown
  • Bookmark the search result so you don't need to re-enter your Manager or League ID in future. You can also share that link with your friends so that they can bypass the search and go straight to the results page
  • Every column in the main tables can be sorted to quickly find the highest/lowest values for any category

Manager Stats

  • Click on the Manager's name in the top left corner to go to that manager's team page on the main FPL website
  • Click on the week number in any of the main tables to see the manager's team for that week on the main FPL website
  • Under 'Team Selection' you can click on the number in the Max Points column to see the optimal team lineup for that week

League Stats

  • Click on the League name in the top left corner to go to that league's page on the main FPL website
  • You can click on a Manager's name in any of the the main tables to go to the Manager Stats view for that person

  • Which Chip was played, if any.

Squad Statistics

Players

Player Name

  • Name of any player who has featured in the matchday squad.

Weeks on Pitch

  • Number of times the player has featured on the pitch, including times where they were selected on the bench but were substituted in.

Weeks in Squad

  • Number of times the player has featured in the matchday squad.

Weeks Captain

  • Number of times the player has acted as Captain, including when they were chosen as Vice Captain and ended up as Captain due to the original Captain not playing.

Points

  • How many points the player has contributed to the total points. Points are only counted if the player was on the pitch and the multiplier for the Captain is included.

% of Total

  • The percentage of total points earned by the player. Total points does not include points deducted for transfers.

Points/Game

  • Average points scored per game. Points are only counted if the player was on the pitch and games where the player was on the bench aren't included in the average.

V.A.P.M.

  • Value Added Per Million pounds of value, an indication of value for money.
    If we assume that every player gets 2 points for minutes played, their added value is any points above or below 2 points and their V.A.P.M. is their average value per game divided by their cost.
    For example, a player who costs 8.0 and scores 8 points adds 6 value. They have an average Points Per Million (P.P.M.) of 1.0 and a V.A.P.M. of 0.75. A player who costs 4.0 and scores 4 points adds 2 value. They also have an average P.P.M. of 1.0 but their V.A.P.M. is only 0.5. Thus, V.A.P.M. accounts for a bias towards cheaper players which occurs if we only consider a total points per game average.
    The price paid by a manager for a player is private, therefore the value used for each player is their average market value across the weeks they've been in the team. As a result, the V.A.P.M. may be slightly innacurate if this value differs from what the manager paid for the player.


Teams

PL Team

  • Each team in the Premier League

Unique Players

  • Number of different players that have been selected in the matchday squad from each team.

Players Started

  • Cumulative number of players who were on the pitch from each team. For example, if you have 2 Spurs players on the pitch for GW1 and 3 Spurs players on the pitch for GW2, your total players started from Spurs after two weeks is 5.

Points

  • How many points players from this team have contributed to the total points. Points are only counted if the player was on the pitch and the multiplier for the Captain is included.

% of Total

  • The percentage of total points earned by players from this team. Total points does not include points deducted for transfers.
